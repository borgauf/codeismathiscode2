** Where is math?

Not /what is math?/, rather, /where is math?/ Once upon a time
(twentieth century on back), math was in books, papers, on chalk
boards. And you could pick up, for instance, an electronic device and
you knew there's math that went into the gizmo's design. And since
caveman days math has been floating around the mysterious neural
networks of our human brains. But now math is on computers. We're on
track to have three computing devices on the planet for every
human. And each of these digital devices is doing math---some simple,
some complex.

Textbook math has long been designed to be tractable with pencil and
paper---and during my lifetime with pocket calculators. But starting
in the 1950s came computers---and nothing really changed at
first. Universities and research organizations all stampeded over to
the digital, Boolean-algebra-based silicon brains, creating their
shadow world of /numerical-computational/ math. But again, the
everyday world---especially K-12 education---trudged along with texts,
pencil, paper, finally adding "graphing calculators."

And yet computerized math was making great inroads in the business
world ... in the unlikely guise of Microsoft's /Excel/ spreadsheet
software. As Excel use increased over the decades, offices everywhere
were doing math on the computer---however specialized that math might
