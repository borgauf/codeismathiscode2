Getting started part 1

Abstractions

Objects alone or together, formalizing things

Objects by themselves or objects together. No doubt many life forms have an instinctual, intuitive, working grasp of these two basic contrasting situations. We humans have language, and this brings logic and symbolism to intuition. Consider the statement

together \(\implies\) not alone, not separate

The \(\implies\) arrow is from mathematical logic and means implies, or, roughly, “if you say this, then you are also saying this.” Reading the statement backwards

not alone, not separate \(\implies\) together

we notice a simple “if not this then that” situation. In both statements we can decide whether they are true or not. In math logic this is called a proposition.

  1. I like you.
  2. Hand me that bottle.
  3. Did you just get here?

Out of these three sentences only #1 is a true proposition, or, a real statement that can be resolved to true or false. #2 is a command, and #3 is a question. Of course #2 and #3 can be answered with yes or no, but not true or false.1 There is a story of the famous physicist Paul Dirac who, finishing a lecture, asked the students if there were any questions. A student said, “I don’t understand how you got…” But Dirac seemingly ignored the student. A professor then asked Dirac if he would answer the question. Dirac replied, Was there a question? I only heard a statement.

Above, together \(\implies\) not alone, not separate is a statement that is either true or false. We might simply believe it to be true, but it may not be. Lurking somewhere might be a situation where together does not really imply or mean not alone and not separate. One case may be riding on a city bus. Yes, you can technically be together with all the other passengers, but still quite alone and separate from all the people you do not know.

Aristotelian logic as put forth by Plato’s student Aristotle in the third century BC begins with three rules

  1. Identity: A thing is itself, or, \(A \text{is} A\)
  2. Excluded middle: A proposition is either true or false, or, \(A \or \not A\)
  3. Contradiction:

, i.e., negation, i.e., not alone, as well as not separate would be together. A negation of yes is no and a negation of positive is negative.2 You have no doubt heard of double negatives where “It’s not that I don’t like you” (I do like you) or “There is no way you are not going to clean your room” (you are going to clean your room).

If together, how many?

Abstraction and symbolism have taken humanity into a modern science and technology landscape that, as far as we know, is unique in the known universe.

things that were together, not just singular by themselves? We would see more than one thing—perhaps similar, perhaps close to one another—and the notion of relatedness, togetherness, clumpiness would come to us. Human language Some vague idea, if not language, dealing with amount rose from how big the clump of things we mentally clumped together seemed.

The general phenomenon of singular and plural, one or more, seemed to beg the question if more, then how many more? And so the size or degree of a clump of things means something and should be considered.

Along with clumpiness, groupings, stuff together, we probably also were aware of the passing, the demarcation of time, such as the seasons cycling. And shapes could be categorized as well.3 Geometry. But let’s stick with clumpiness for a while.

Communication, semantics of these clump details evolved. A couple, a pair, a herd, a school, a few, many, lots, lots and lots, lots and lots and lots. And today terms like cardinality or degrees of multiplicity offer a high level of abstraction of one or many.

All of this points to a very unique thing about humans, namely, our high levels of cognizance, awareness, our ability to note our intuitive awareness and build on it. Of course other critters are able to distinguish between one and many. But do they put these notions, these impulses into words—and then build these ideas into higher and more exacting and more abstract ideas? Of course humans experience life at an intuitive level, but unlike animals, we take the intuitive and give it logical structure, we codify these notions, ideas, intuitive hunches. We, far more than any other species take basic phenomena and create abstract representative mental structures.
